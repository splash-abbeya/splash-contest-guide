Step 1 of 5
Get Started on Splash
▾

When you first land on Splash Sports, you'll see the home page with trending contests, League Lobbies, and QuickPicks. Creating a new contest works the same way no matter which sport you want to run — your starting point is always in the top right corner.

🖥️
Find the "+ Create contest" button

It's the teal button in the top-right corner of the navigation bar — you'll see it on every page once you're logged in.

👆
Click "+ Create contest"

This opens the Contest Setup flow. Don't worry — nothing is created until you click the final "Create contest" button at the end.

💡 New to Splash? Make sure you're logged in first. If you don't have an account yet, sign up at app.splashsports.com — it's free to join and create contests.
2
Step 2 of 5
Pick Your League
▾

The first screen shows a grid of available sports leagues. Pick the sport you want to run a contest for — each league has its own set of contest formats tailored to that sport.

🏟️
Select your league from the grid

Click any league tile — it highlights with a teal border to confirm your selection. Tiles with a grey eye icon are currently unavailable for new contests (usually out of season).

▶️
Click "Continue"

The Continue button activates once you've selected a league. After clicking, the contest type options for that sport will appear — the next step covers all formats by league.

💡 Available leagues on Splash NFL, PGA, NBA, MLB, NCAAB, and NCAAF each have their own contest formats — Survivor, Pick'Em, One & Done, Tiers, and more. The setup flow is the same no matter which sport you pick; the format options just change to match.
3
Step 3 of 5
Choose Your Contest Type
▾

After selecting your league, you'll choose a contest type. The options vary by sport — here's a plain-English breakdown of every format, organized by league.

🏈 NFL
Survivor
Pick one NFL team to win each week. If your team loses, you're eliminated. Last person standing wins. Classic and easy to explain to new players.
⭐ Most popular
Pick'Em
Pick the winner of every game each week. Whoever gets the most right wins. Simple, season-long, great for big groups.
Pick'Em: Confidence
Same as Pick'Em but you assign confidence points to each pick — more points on your sure bets, fewer on the coin flips. Adds strategy.
Player Survivor
Like Survivor, but instead of picking a team, you pick an individual player to perform each week. Can't reuse the same player twice.
Tiers: Fantasy Points
Pick players from pre-set tiers (like a salary cap) each week. Scored by fantasy points. Less lineup-heavy than traditional DFS.
One & Done: Fantasy Points / Touchdowns
Pick one player per week, can never reuse them. Scored by fantasy points or touchdowns depending on the variant. Great for season-long engagement.
NFL Playoffs — Tiers / Pick'Em - Playoffs / Playoffs Survivor
Playoff-specific versions of Tiers, Pick'Em, and Survivor. Run a quick contest just for the postseason — great if you don't want a full season commitment.
Squares
The classic Super Bowl squares grid. Assign squares randomly and pay out based on the score at the end of each quarter.
SB Props
Super Bowl prop picks — answer yes/no or multiple choice questions about Super Bowl outcomes. Fun and low-effort for casual fans.
⛳ PGA
One & Done formats
One & Done — Dollar Winnings
Each week, pick one golfer to represent you in that tournament. Once you use a golfer, you can't pick them again all season. Standings are based on how much prize money your chosen golfer earns. At the end of the season, the top finishers in your contest win real dollar prizes.
⭐ Best for beginners
One & Done — Total Strokes
Same "one golfer per tournament, no repeats" format — but scored by how many total strokes your golfer shoots across the season, not by their prize earnings. Lower strokes = better.
Golf Majors Tiers formats
Golf Majors Tiers — Dollar Winnings
Pick a team of golfers from pre-set tiers for each of the four Major tournaments. Scored by prize money earned. Good for groups who only want to compete during the biggest events of the year.
Golf Majors Tiers — Total Strokes
Same tier-based team picks for Majors only, but scored by cumulative strokes instead of prize money.
Multi-Tournament Tiers formats
Multi-Tournament Tiers — Dollar Winnings
Pick a team of golfers from tiers across multiple selected tournaments throughout the season. Scored by prize money. Best for long-running leagues.
Multi-Tournament Tiers — Total Strokes
Same multi-tournament tier format, but scored by stroke totals across the tournaments you include.
Single Tournament Tiers formats
Single Tournament Tiers — Dollar Winnings
Pick a team of golfers from tiers for one specific tournament only. Scored by prize money earned that week. Great for one-off contests around a marquee event.
Single Tournament Tiers — Total Strokes
Same single-tournament tier format, scored by stroke total for that event. Perfect for one-and-done groups who want a quick contest around a specific week.

🏀 NBA
NBA Tiers
Pick players from pre-set tiers each week. Scored by fantasy points. Great for NBA fans who want a lighter lift than traditional DFS.
⚾ MLB
Player Survivor
Pick one MLB player to perform each week — can't reuse them. If your player doesn't perform up to the threshold, you're out. Season-long elimination format.
13-Run
A unique MLB format — pick a team and bet on whether they'll score 13 or more runs in a game. High-risk, fun, and perfect for baseball fans.
🏀 NCAAB (College Basketball)
NCAAB Men's Bracket / Women's Bracket
Fill out a March Madness bracket and score points for every correct pick. The classic tournament format — best for March Madness season.
⭐ March Madness
NCAAB Survivor — Pick by Day / Pick by Round
Pick one team to win each day (or each round). Team loses = you're eliminated. Two variants: daily picks or one pick per tournament round.
NCAAB Pick'Em
Pick the winner of every college basketball game. Whoever gets the most right across the tournament wins. Great for big groups.
NCAAB Squares
Classic squares grid format applied to college basketball. Payout by score at end of each quarter or half.
🏈 NCAAF (College Football)
CFB Playoffs Survivor
Survivor format for the College Football Playoff. Pick one team to win each round — lose and you're out. Quick and exciting for playoff season.
College Bowl Pick'Em
Pick winners across all the college bowl games. Best for the bowl season (December–January). Easy to run with a big group of college football fans.
Pick'Em / Pick'Em: Confidence
Pick the winner of every college football game each week, with optional confidence point weighting. Same as the NFL format applied to college.
Survivor
Pick one college team to win each week. If they lose, you're eliminated. Season-long format — good for dedicated CFB fans.
Squares
Classic squares grid applied to college football. Perfect for big rivalry games or bowl games with casual fans.
⚠️ Common mistake: mixing up "Dollar Winnings" vs. "Total Strokes"

Dollar Winnings rewards your golfer's finishing position (prize purse earned). Total Strokes counts the actual number of shots — lower is better. Make sure your whole group agrees on which scoring system you're using before you create the contest.

✅
Select your format, then click Continue

Click your chosen format to highlight it with a teal border, then click Continue to move to the setup form.

Step 4 of 5
Set Up Your Contest
▾

Now you're on the Contest Setup form. This has several sections — here's what each one does and what you actually need to fill out.

Contest Title Give your contest a name your friends will recognize — e.g. "Mike's PGA One & Done 2025"
Required
Contest Description Add house rules, payout notes, or anything your players need to know. Totally optional.
Optional
Contest Banner Upload a custom image (2880×170px, PNG/JPG, max 4MB). Skip this for now — you can always add it later.
Optional
Sport & Game Style Pre-filled with your selected league and format from the previous steps. No action needed — just confirm it looks right.
Auto-filled
Schedule Defaults to "All Events." Click to expand if you want to exclude specific tournaments or games.
Review
Entry Fee Click "Set up" to configure how much each player pays to enter and how payouts are structured.
Set up
Contest Size Click "Set up" to set a max number of players. This controls how many invites can be accepted.
Set up
General / Advanced / Admin Settings Click "Review" on each to see the defaults. Most first-time commissioners can leave these as-is.
Review
⚠️ Don't skip Entry Fee & Contest Size

These two are the most commonly missed settings. If you skip them, your contest may not work correctly when players try to join. Click "Set up" on both before creating your contest.

🙋 Prefer to handle money yourself? Choose "I'll collect entries and handle payouts on my own" in the Entry Fee setup. Players won't pay through Splash — you collect cash, Venmo, or however you like. There's no entry fee charged to your players, but a commissioner service fee still applies to host the contest on Splash.
🚀
Click "Create contest" when ready

The big teal button at the bottom. This is the final step — your contest goes live immediately and you'll land on the Manage Contest dashboard.

Step 5 of 5
Invite Your Friends
▾

Your contest is live! Now you're on the Manage Contest dashboard. Head to the Invites Manager to get your friends in. There are several ways to invite people:

📋
Click "Invites Manager" in the left sidebar

Or click the Invites Manager card in the main dashboard area. Both take you to the same place.

🔗 Share link (easiest)

Copy your contest invite link and paste it anywhere — text, email, group chat, social media. Anyone with the link can join.

👥 Followers

Invite people who already follow you on Splash. Great if your friends are already on the platform.

📧 Import

Upload a list of email addresses to invite people who aren't on Splash yet. They'll get an email to sign up and join.

🏈 Previous entrants

Re-invite people from contests you've run before. Perfect for recurring leagues.

⚠️ Heads up — invites may go to spam

Splash's invitation emails sometimes land in spam folders, especially for new users. Ask your friends to search their email for "noreply@splashsports.com" and mark it as "Not Spam" so they don't miss future contest communications.

💡 Track your invites in the "Sent" tab After sending invites, click the Sent tab to see who's been invited, who's entered, and who's still pending. You can send a reminder directly from there.
🎉

Your contest is live!

You've completed all five steps. Share your invite link, sit back, and let the competition begin. Good luck, commissioner!
