🏈 NFL 9 formats available ▾
▶ Survivor ⭐ Most Popular

Pick one NFL team to win each week. You can't use the same team twice all season. If your pick loses, you're eliminated. Last player standing wins the pool.

Why choose thisThe most played NFL format on Splash. Simple to explain in 30 seconds, strategic enough to debate every Sunday. Everyone stays invested all season because one bad week ends your run.
Any group sizeAny experience levelFull seasonElimination format
▶ Pick'Em ⭐ Great for Large Groups

Pick the winners of every game each week. Correct picks earn points. The player with the most correct picks at season's end wins. No elimination — everyone plays all season.

Why choose thisIdeal for large groups (25+) where you don't want anyone dropping out early. The leaderboard stays competitive all season, and even casual fans can win a week by going 10-for-10.
Large groups (25+)Mixed experienceFull seasonNo elimination
▶ Pick'Em: Confidence

Same as Pick'Em but you assign confidence points to each game pick — 1 through the number of games that week. Your highest-confidence picks earn the most points if correct, but cost the most if wrong.

Why choose thisAdds a skill layer that rewards football knowledge, not just luck. Separates sharp pickers from casual ones. A weekly upset can flip the leaderboard in a single afternoon.
Competitive groupsFantasy veteransMedium–large groupsFull season
▶ Player Survivor

Pick one NFL player to hit a stat threshold each week (e.g., 100+ rushing yards, 250+ passing yards). You can't use the same player twice. Miss the threshold and you're eliminated.

Why choose thisA player-focused twist on the classic Survivor formula. Perfect for fantasy football fans who love tracking individual player performance rather than just team wins.
Fantasy football fans10–50 playersFull seasonElimination format
▶ Tiers: Fantasy Points

Pick players from pre-defined tiers each week and earn points based on their fantasy performance. A new lineup every week — no elimination, pure accumulation.

Why choose thisGreat for groups who want DFS-lite without the daily burnout. No elimination pressure means everyone stays engaged all season, and fresh weekly lineups keep it interesting.
Fantasy regulars10–50 playersFull seasonNo elimination
▶ One & Done

Pick one team to win each week. Each team can only be used once all season. Track your record — the most wins at season's end wins the pool. No elimination.

Why choose thisLike Survivor without the elimination stress. Good teams are used up as the season goes on, forcing increasingly creative picks later. Great for groups who want strategy without the "you're out" drama.
Any group sizeCasual–mixedFull seasonNo elimination
▶ Playoffs Formats

Bracket-style or Survivor-style picks specifically for the NFL Playoffs — from Wild Card Weekend through the Super Bowl. Short, intense, and high-stakes.

Why choose thisPerfect if your group doesn't want a full-season commitment. Launch when the playoff bracket is set in January and run through the Super Bowl. Great standalone or add-on contest.
Any group sizeJanuary–February onlyShort season
▶ Squares

A classic 10×10 grid game. Players claim squares, and winners are determined by the last digit of each team's score at the end of each quarter.

Why choose thisNo football knowledge required — pure fun. Perfect for Super Bowl parties where you have non-football fans in the room. Everyone has a stake in every score change.
Any groupParty formatSingle gameCasual-friendly
▶ SB Props

Players predict specific Super Bowl prop outcomes — coin flip, first score, MVP, halftime show moments, and more. Pure fun, no deep football knowledge needed.

Why choose thisGreat companion to your main season contest. Very accessible — casual fans and non-football watchers can compete on coin flips and celebrity appearances alongside your fantasy veterans.
Any groupSuper Bowl onlyCasual-friendly

⛳ PGA 5 formats available ▾
▶ One & Done — Dollar Winnings ⭐ Most Popular

Pick one golfer per tournament to follow all season. You can't use the same golfer twice. Players are scored by their official prize money earnings — the more your golfer wins, the better your score.

Why choose thisSplash's most popular PGA format. Strategy builds over the season — save your top-tier golfers for the majors, or ride a hot streak early. Big prize events mean big leaderboard swings.
Golf fans of all levels10–50 playersFull seasonNo elimination
▶ One & Done — Strokes

Same One & Done mechanic — one golfer per week, can't repeat — but scored by strokes gained or finishing position instead of prize money.

Why choose thisBetter for groups more comfortable reading leaderboards than prize money totals. Finishing position is easy to follow live and doesn't require understanding payout distributions.
Casual golf fansSmaller groupsFull season
▶ Golf Majors Tiers

Pick golfers from pre-set tiers specifically for the four major championships: The Masters, PGA Championship, US Open, and The Open. Short, focused, and high-prestige.

Why choose thisPerfect if your group only tunes in for the majors. Four events, four rounds of drama. Run it alongside your NFL Survivor or as a standalone spring/summer golf contest.
Casual–mixed8–40 playersMajors onlyShort commitment
▶ Multi-Tournament Tiers

Season-long tier selections across multiple PGA Tour events. Pick from tiered golfer pools each tournament and accumulate fantasy points as the season progresses.

Why choose thisThe deepest PGA format on Splash. Rewards golfers who follow the tour week-to-week, not just during the majors. Ideal for serious golf groups who want consistent weekly engagement.
Hardcore golf fansSmaller competitive groupsFull season
▶ Single Tournament Tiers

Tier picks for one specific PGA Tour event. Pick your lineup from tiered golfer pools and track your points across four days of competition.

Why choose thisZero season-long commitment. Perfect for a single big event — launch a Masters or US Open contest, watch the event, done. Great for bringing in groups who wouldn't commit to a full season.
Any group sizeCasual fansSingle eventLow commitment
🏀 NCAAB — College Basketball 6 formats available ▾
▶ Men's Bracket ⭐ March Madness

Pick the winner of every game in the NCAA Men's Basketball Tournament. Score points for each correct pick, with higher-value points in later rounds. Fill it out after Selection Sunday and watch it play out over three weeks.

Why choose thisThe most well-known contest format in all of sports. Literally everyone knows how a bracket works. Perfect for any office pool, friend group, or family — including people who don't watch basketball the rest of the year.
Any groupAny sizeMarch–April onlyNo experience needed
▶ Women's Bracket ⭐ Growing Fast

Same bracket format as the men's tournament but for the NCAA Women's Basketball Tournament. Runs concurrently with the men's bracket in March and April.

Why choose thisRun both brackets simultaneously for double the engagement. Women's tournament parity often makes the bracket harder to predict — which can mean a more competitive result. Great for groups with fans of the women's game.
Any groupAny sizeMarch–April only
▶ Survivor — Pick by Day

Pick one team to win on a given day during the tournament. Each day of March Madness has multiple games — pick one winner, can't reuse teams. Miss and you're eliminated.

Why choose thisMore frequent decisions than Pick by Round — you're picking every day of the tournament instead of waiting for a full round to finish. Great for groups who want daily engagement across the three-week event.
Competitive groups10–40 playersMarch–April onlyDaily picks
▶ Survivor — Pick by Round

Pick one team to survive each round of March Madness. If your team loses in any round, you're eliminated. No upfront bracket — fresh pick each round. Last player standing wins.

Why choose thisMore strategic than filling out a full bracket. Forces deliberate round-by-round decisions. High drama in the final rounds when only a few players remain — great for competitive groups.
Competitive groups10–30 playersMarch–April only
▶ Pick'Em

Pick game winners each week throughout the college basketball regular season. Season-long point accumulation. No elimination — everyone plays all year.

Why choose thisGreat for groups who follow college basketball beyond just March Madness. Season-long engagement from November through the conference tournaments in early March.
CBB fansAny group sizeNovember–March
