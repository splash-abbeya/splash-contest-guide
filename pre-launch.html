💰 Entry Fee & Payout Type
✓
I clicked "Set up" on Entry Fee — it is not blank The most commonly missed step. Skipping it means your contest may not collect fees. It won't be obvious until players try to join. ⚠️ Most commonly skipped step
✓
I confirmed my entry fee amount with my group before setting it Agree on the dollar amount with your players before you create the contest. Entry fees cannot be changed once players have joined.
✓
I chose Splash-managed or self-managed payouts Splash-managed = players pay through the app, Splash pays winners automatically. Self-managed = you collect and distribute money yourself (set the fee to $0 in Splash).
✓
My group agreed on the payout structure before I created the contest How much does 1st place get? Is there a 2nd or 3rd place payout? Agree on splits upfront to avoid disputes at season's end.
✓
I understand the commissioner service fee and have accounted for it Splash charges a commissioner fee for running the contest. Review the exact amount during setup before confirming creation.
👥 Contest Size & Access
✓
I clicked "Set up" on Contest Size — it is not blank Another commonly skipped step. If left blank, your contest may not limit entries or may not allow players to join at all. ⚠️ Commonly skipped step
✓
The max player count is set higher than my expected group size Set it 10–20% above your expected headcount. You can always add more players later, but reaching the cap blocks new joiners.
✓
I set the entry deadline (or confirmed the default is appropriate) Some formats lock new entries before the first game. Check when your contest closes to new players and make sure it's communicated.
✓
I set contest visibility correctly (public vs. private) Private = invite-only, won't appear in public search. Public = anyone can find and join. Most commissioner groups should be set to private.
⚙️ Format-Specific Settings
✓
I reviewed which weeks, events, or tournaments are included For season-long formats: are all the weeks you want included? For single-event formats: is the right event selected?
✓
I reviewed pick deadline timing When do picks lock each week/round? Most formats lock picks before the first game of the period. Confirm your players know this deadline.
✓
I reviewed format-specific settings (confidence scale, tier structure, stat thresholds) Pick'Em: Confidence needs a point scale. Tiers needs tier groupings. Player Survivor needs a stat threshold. Check the advanced settings for your format.
✓
I reviewed what happens when a player misses their picks In Survivor, missing a week often counts as a loss. In Pick'Em, it's 0 points. Confirm your contest's missed-pick rule and communicate it to your group.
📏 Rules & Tiebreakers
✓
I reviewed the tiebreaker rules for my contest What happens if two players finish with the same score? Check Contest Settings → General Settings → Tiebreaker to see how it's resolved.
✓
I can explain the contest rules clearly to a new player If you can't summarize the rules in 3–4 sentences, you may have questions during the season. Prep a short rules blurb to share with your group.
✓
I know what I'll do if a dispute arises mid-season For scoring disputes, direct players to their entry detail page first. For unresolved issues, Splash support handles disputes — have the support link ready.
📢 Invites & Player Communication
✓
I have my invite link ready and copied before announcing the contest Share the direct join link via text or group chat — it's faster than email and bypasses spam folders entirely.
✓
I told my players the first pick deadline before they join Let them know exactly when picks lock before the first game. This is the #1 cause of "I missed the deadline" complaints from new players.
✓
I warned players to check spam for Splash emails Ask them to search for noreply@splashsports.com and mark as "Not Spam." Otherwise invite emails and pick reminders may never reach them.
✓
I drafted a launch message to send my group when the contest goes live Include: the join link, entry fee amount, first pick deadline, and one-sentence format summary. Players shouldn't have to ask for basics.
✓
I considered sharing the Player Quick-Start Guide with new players If any players are new to Splash, the Player Guide walks them through account creation, joining, making picks, and tracking results.
