Invites & Joining
6 issues
▾
▶ My invite email went to spam — what should I tell my players?

This is the most common issue new Splash players encounter. Ask them to search their inbox (including Spam/Junk) for noreply@splashsports.com and mark it as "Not Spam." Future emails will land in their inbox.

💡 Share the contest invite link directly via text or group chat instead — it bypasses email entirely and is the fastest way to get players into the contest.
▶ A player can't join using the invite link — what's wrong?

A few things to check:

1. Contest is full — if you set a max player limit and it's been reached, no one else can join. Go to the contest settings and increase the Contest Size.

2. Entry deadline passed — some contest types lock entries before the first game. If the deadline has passed, new players can't join.

3. Player isn't logged in — the link requires a Splash account. If they don't have one, clicking the link will prompt them to sign up first.

▶ How do I resend an invite or send a reminder?

Go to your contest dashboard → Invites Manager → click the Sent tab. You'll see everyone who was invited and their status. Click Remind next to anyone who hasn't entered yet to send a follow-up email.

▶ Can I invite someone after the contest has started?

It depends on the contest type. For some formats (like Survivor or Pick'Em), you can invite additional players after the contest starts, but they'll join mid-season. For formats with a single entry window (like Squares or Bracket), the window closes once the event begins.

⚠️ Always check your contest's entry deadline in the contest settings before sending late invites.
▶ A player joined but I don't see them in the contest — where are they?

Check the Invites Manager → Accepted tab. They may have joined but not yet submitted their picks. A player who accepted the invite but hasn't made picks will appear in Accepted but not on the leaderboard until picks are submitted.

▶ How do I remove a player from my contest?

Go to your contest dashboard → Manage Players. Find the player and select Remove. Note: if they paid an entry fee through Splash, a refund may need to be processed separately. Reach out to Splash support if a paid entry is involved.

Picks & Entries
5 issues
▾
▶ A player says they can't make their picks — what should I check?

1. Pick deadline has passed — most contest types lock picks before the first game of the week/round. Check the contest's pick deadline in the settings.

2. They haven't accepted the invite — players must accept the invite and have an active entry before they can submit picks.

3. Wrong sport/week selected — in multi-week formats, players need to make picks for the current active week, not a past one.

▶ A player's picks didn't save — what do they do?

Ask them to try the following:

1. Refresh the page and check if their picks appear — sometimes picks save but don't visually confirm immediately.

2. Try a different browser or clear their browser cache.

3. Try on the Splash mobile app instead of the web browser.

⚠️ If the pick deadline has already passed, picks cannot be retroactively submitted. Encourage players to submit picks early.
▶ Can a player change their pick after submitting?

Yes — picks can be changed any time before the pick deadline. Once the deadline passes and games begin, picks are locked and cannot be changed. Remind players to double-check before the deadline closes.

▶ A player forgot to submit picks — are they eliminated?

It depends on your contest type and settings. In Survivor, missing a week's pick typically counts as a loss. In Pick'Em, missing picks for a week means 0 points for that round. Check your specific contest's rules under Contest Settings → General Settings for how missed picks are handled.

▶ Can I make picks on behalf of a player?

No — commissioners cannot submit picks on behalf of other players. Each player must log in to their own account to make picks. This is by design to ensure fairness.

Entry Fees & Payments
6 issues
▾
▶ What is the commissioner service fee and why is it charged?

The commissioner service fee is charged to the contest creator (you) for hosting the contest on Splash's platform. It covers the infrastructure, scoring, and tools that Splash provides — regardless of whether players pay an entry fee through Splash or you collect money yourself.

💡 The fee amount varies by contest type and size. You can see the exact fee before finalizing your contest setup.
▶ What's the difference between Splash-managed and self-managed payouts?

Splash-managed: Players pay their entry fee directly through Splash. Splash holds the pool and pays out winners automatically when the contest ends.

Self-managed: You set a $0 entry fee through Splash. You collect money from players yourself (cash, Venmo, etc.) and handle payouts manually. The commissioner service fee still applies to you, but your players pay nothing through Splash.

▶ A player was charged but says they can't access the contest — what do I do?

First, check that the player appears in your Invites Manager → Accepted tab. If they're there, they have access — walk them through finding the contest on their account dashboard.

If they paid but don't appear in your contest at all, contact Splash support directly at support@splashsports.com with the player's account email and the contest name.

▶ How long does it take for winnings to be paid out?

For Splash-managed contests, payouts are typically processed within 3–5 business days after the contest ends and results are finalized. Players will receive an email notification when their payout is processed.

💡 Players need a verified payment method on their Splash account to receive payouts. Remind winners to check their account settings if they don't see their payout.
▶ Can I get a refund if I cancel my contest?

Refund eligibility depends on whether the contest has started and how many players have joined. Contact Splash support at support@splashsports.com as soon as possible — refunds are handled on a case-by-case basis.

⚠️ Commissioner service fees are generally non-refundable once a contest is live and players have joined.
▶ Can I change the entry fee after the contest is created?

No — once a contest is live and players have joined, the entry fee cannot be changed. If no players have joined yet, you may be able to cancel and recreate the contest with the correct fee. Contact Splash support if you need help.

Scoring & Results
5 issues
▾
▶ Scores aren't updating — is there a delay?

Yes — Splash scores update in near real-time but there can be a lag of 5–15 minutes during live games. If scores haven't updated several hours after a game ended, try refreshing the page. If it's still stuck, the data feed may be delayed — it usually self-corrects within a few hours.

▶ A player thinks their score is wrong — what do I tell them?

Direct them to check their entry detail page inside the contest — it shows exactly what picks were recorded and how each one scored. This is the source of truth.

If a score still looks wrong after the game has been final for 24 hours, contact Splash support with the contest name, player username, and the specific game/week in question.

▶ When do final results post after the season ends?

Final results are typically posted within 24–48 hours after the last game of the contest is played. For season-long contests, this happens after the final week's games are complete. Players receive an email when final results are available.

▶ What happens if a game is postponed or cancelled?

Splash follows official league rules for postponed or cancelled games. Typically:

Postponed games are treated as pending until rescheduled, and picks are carried over or voided depending on the contest type.

Cancelled games are usually voided — picks for that game don't count toward scoring either way.

💡 Check the specific contest's rules page for how your contest format handles cancelled games.
▶ There's a tie — how are tiebreakers handled?

Tiebreaker rules vary by contest type. Some formats include a built-in tiebreaker (like a confidence point system or a specific tiebreaker question). If no tiebreaker is configured and a tie occurs, the prize is typically split between tied players. Check your contest's General Settings for the specific tiebreaker rule that was set at creation.

Contest Settings & Editing
5 issues
▾
▶ Can I change the contest format after it's been created?

No — the contest format (e.g. Survivor, Pick'Em, One & Done) cannot be changed once the contest is live. If you selected the wrong format, you'll need to cancel the contest and create a new one. Contact Splash support if players have already joined and paid.

▶ Can I change the contest name or description after creating it?

Yes — go to your contest dashboard → Contest Settings → General. You can edit the contest name, description, and banner image at any time, even after players have joined.

▶ How do I increase the maximum number of players?

Go to Contest Settings → Contest Size and update the max player limit. You can increase this at any time as long as the contest is still accepting entries. Decreasing below the current number of joined players is not allowed.

▶ How do I cancel a contest?

Go to your contest dashboard → Contest Settings → Advanced Settings → scroll to the bottom for the cancel option. If players have joined and paid, cancelling will trigger refunds — contact Splash support before cancelling if this is the case to ensure the process goes smoothly.

⚠️ Cancellations cannot be undone. Make sure you want to cancel before confirming.
▶ I accidentally set the wrong schedule (wrong weeks / tournaments) — can I fix it?

Schedule changes after contest creation are generally not supported once players have joined. If no players have joined yet, try editing under Contest Settings → Schedule. If that option isn't available or players have already entered, contact Splash support as soon as possible.

Account & Login
4 issues
▾
▶ A player can't log into their Splash account — what do they do?

Direct them to app.splashsports.com and use the Forgot Password link on the login screen. They'll receive a reset email. If the reset email doesn't arrive, ask them to check their spam folder.

▶ A player created an account but can't find the contest — how do they locate it?

Once logged in, they should check My Contests in the navigation. If the contest doesn't appear there, they may have signed up with a different email than the one the invite was sent to. Ask them to check which email they used to sign up vs. which email received the invite.

▶ A player has two Splash accounts — can they merge them?

Account merges aren't self-service. They need to contact Splash support at support@splashsports.com with both account email addresses to request a merge.

▶ How do I transfer commissioner ownership of a contest to someone else?

Commissioner transfer isn't currently a self-service feature. Contact Splash support with the contest name and the new commissioner's account email to request the transfer.

Commissioner Dashboard
4 issues
▾
▶ Where do I find my contest after creating it?

After creating a contest, you land on the Manage Contest dashboard automatically. To find it later, log in and go to My Contests in the top navigation — your contest will appear under "Contests I Manage."

▶ How do I see who has entered vs. who hasn't submitted picks yet?

From the contest dashboard, go to Invites Manager → Accepted to see who has joined. Check the leaderboard to see who has submitted picks for the current week — players who joined but haven't picked won't appear on the leaderboard yet.

▶ Can I post a message or announcement to all players in my contest?

Splash doesn't currently have a built-in announcement feature. The best approach is to use the contest's invite link to get players into a group chat (text thread, WhatsApp, GroupMe, Discord) where you can communicate directly with everyone.

▶ The season ended — how do I officially close the contest?

For most contest types, Splash closes the contest automatically when the final game is scored and results are finalized. You don't need to do anything manually. For self-managed payouts, you'll need to distribute winnings to players yourself once you know the final standings.
