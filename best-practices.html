💡 Commissioner Playbook

Run a Contest People Come Back To

The difference between a one-and-done contest and a league that's been running for 10 years is almost always the commissioner. Here's what the best ones do.

🧱 Before You Launch Setup
🎯

Pick the right format for your group — not the most complex one

A Survivor pool with 8 friends who've never played fantasy before will outperform a Tiers contest where no one knows the scoring. Match the format to the room. Use the Contest Picker if you're unsure.

💬

Agree on the rules before you create the contest

Entry fee, payout structure, what happens with ties, missed picks — get group agreement before you launch. The Payout Calculator helps. A quick group text goes a long way.

📅

Create 1–2 weeks before the season, not the night before

Players need time to join, create accounts if they're new, and understand the format. Rushing the setup leads to missed first-week picks — the most common commissioner complaint.

📢

Use the share link, not email invites

Splash invite emails land in spam for first-time players more often than you'd think. Text the invite link to your group — it's instant, works on any device, and doesn't require an email account.

📣 Getting Players In Recruitment
⏰

Set a firm join deadline and announce it upfront

Tell people: "You need to join by Sunday at noon or you're out." A deadline creates urgency and prevents you from chasing stragglers all week. Use the Sent tab in Invites Manager to see who hasn't joined.

📱

Send the Player Quick-Start Guide to new Splash users

New players don't know how to find the contest, make picks, or what to do when they win. Send them the Player Guide — it answers every question they'll ask you in the first week.

🎁

Lower the barrier for hesitant players

Someone on the fence about joining? Offer a $0 entry for the first week to let them try it out. Or run a free contest the first year to build the habit — paid entries work better once people are hooked.

👥

Create a group chat specifically for the contest

A dedicated text thread, WhatsApp group, or Discord channel is your communication layer for the whole season. It's where you share reminders, trash talk, and results. It's what makes a contest feel like a league.

🔥 Keeping It Alive Mid-Season Engagement
🔔

Send a pick reminder every week — without exception

A Tuesday or Wednesday message in the group chat: "Picks lock Sunday at 1pm ET — don't forget." People are busy. Even veterans miss picks because life happens. The reminder is the single highest-ROI commissioner action.

📊

Share the leaderboard at the halfway point

Post a screenshot of the standings at the midpoint of the season. People who are winning get excited. People in the middle feel like they still have a shot. Even people at the bottom start paying more attention.

😂

Let trash talk happen — it's the glue

The group chat banter between picks is half the reason people come back. Celebrate big wins. Roast brutal losses. The more it feels like a community, the more people re-up next year.

🏅

Keep eliminated players engaged

In Survivor, players who get knocked out still care — if they have friends still in. Ask eliminated players to make "shadow picks" in the group chat. Or run a side game (best remaining record) to keep everyone watching.

✅ Do this
  • Send weekly pick reminders
  • Share leaderboard screenshots
  • Acknowledge big wins publicly
  • Set clear deadlines upfront
  • Use the group chat constantly
❌ Avoid this
  • Changing rules mid-season
  • Going silent after launch
  • Letting pick disputes fester
  • Delaying payouts after the contest ends
  • Making the format too complex for the group
⚖️ Handling Disputes & Issues Commissioner Calls
📜

The rules you set at the start are the rules — full stop

When someone disputes a scoring decision or a missed pick, refer back to the rules you agreed on before the season. "It's in the rules we set up" is a complete sentence. Don't make case-by-case exceptions — it always leads to more disputes.

🤝

Handle money disputes fast and transparently

If there's a payout question, share the math publicly in the group chat — exact prize pool, exact split, exact amount per place. Transparency kills 90% of payment disputes before they start.

🚫

Don't override Splash's scoring — it's the source of truth

If a score looks wrong, check the official entry detail page before making any judgment call. Splash pulls from official data feeds. A score that "looks wrong" is almost always correct — the official game stats updated before the broadcaster did.

📞

Know when to escalate to Splash support

Payment issues, platform errors, or anything you can't resolve in the Manage Contest dashboard — contact support@splashsports.com. Don't try to work around platform issues yourself.

🔄 Running It Back Next Year Retention
📋

Do a quick debrief after the season ends

One message in the group chat: "What did everyone think? Should we do this again next year? Any changes?" You'll get honest feedback and social proof that people want to come back — use it.

💰

Pay out fast — same week the contest ends

For self-managed payouts: don't wait. Send Venmo or collect cash at a wrap-up event the same week. The longer you wait, the more it feels like an afterthought. Fast payouts = people who sign up again next year.

🏆

Create a tradition around the champion

A trophy, a plaque, a Twitter/group shoutout, a "Champion" role in Discord — something that marks the winner as different. Bragging rights are often worth more than the prize money. Make the win feel real.

📆

Announce next year's contest before the current one ends

The best time to recruit for next year is while everyone is emotionally invested in the current season. Drop a "who's in for next year?" message in the final weeks — you'll get more sign-ups than any other time.
